Buffon's Needle Problem

Posted on 2019-03-12 Edited on 2019-10-11 In Mathematics , Statistics , Physics

Buffon's Needle Problem is a well known example for Monte Carlo Simulation. it demonstrates the basic idea of Monte Carlo methods throughly Evaluate certain quantities by generating proper random processes.

The setup of problem is quite simple

Suppose we have a floor made of geometrically parallel strips of wood each the same width, and we drop a needle onto the floor. What is the probability that the needle will lie across a line between two strips?

We may also assume that the needle is shorter than the width of the parallel strips, the so-called "short needle case".

Here we denote the width of parallel lines by \(D\) and the length of needle by \(l\). The distance from the midpoint to the nearest line is represented by \(x\) and the angle between the intersected line and the needle is \(\theta\). As we assumed, the following relation must be satisfied: \[ l \leq D \] The condition that the needle will intersect with the line is simply: \[ x \leq \frac{l}{2} \sin \theta \] We denote the probability by \[ \begin{aligned} p &\equiv P\left\{ \text{intersect} \right\} \\ &= P\left\{ x \leq \frac{l}{2} \sin \theta \right\} \\ & = \iint \limits_{ x \leq \frac{l}{2} \sin \theta }{f(x, \theta)} dx d\theta \end{aligned} \] Next we are going to consider the distribution of \(x\) and \(\theta\) as well as their joint distribution. It natural to view them uniformly distributed \[ \begin{aligned} x &\sim U\left[ 0, \frac{D}{2}\right] \\ \theta &\sim U\left[ 0, \frac{\pi}{2}\right] \\ \end{aligned} \] Since \(x\) and \(\theta\) is independent, the joint distribution is simply their product: \[ f(x, \theta) = \frac{4}{\pi D} \] Thus, \[ p = \frac{4}{\pi D} \iint \limits_{ x \leq \frac{l}{2} \sin \theta }dx d\theta \] The next step is to determine the region of integration. Draw a plot of \(x = \frac{l}{2} \sin \theta\) and you will easily attain that. Therefore, \[ \begin{aligned} p &=  \frac{4}{\pi D} \iint \limits_{ x \leq \frac{l}{2} \sin \theta }dx d\theta \\ &= \frac{4}{\pi D} \int \limits_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}d\theta \int \limits_{0}^{\frac{l}{2} \sin \theta}dx \\ &= \frac{2l}{\pi D} \end{aligned} \] As we can approximate the probability by frequency \(\hat{p}\), the estimation of \(\pi\) in our trial is \[ \hat{\pi} = \frac{2 l}{\hat{p}D} \]

Hexo is a extensive blog platform. There are tons of elegant themes for it and you can even create your own one! I use NexT theme for this site. In fact the Hexo official site keeps a collection of themes. In this post I am showing your how to change the default theme to NexT

Get the Theme Package

  • Installing the Latest Version

    Since Git is installed if you followed the tutorial in this blog, cloning the latest version the most convenient way. Change working directory to your local blog folder and get the newest one

  • Installing the Stable or a Custom Version

    However, you are always free to decide which version to install. This is simple as well.

    1. Visit the NexT Release Page.
    2. Download the version you favor.
    3. Make an empty folder named next in theme subdirectory.
    4. Unzip the downloaded assets to the next folder you have just created.

Enable the NexT Theme

  1. Configure Site _comfig.yml File

    Like all Hexo themes, after you download it, open site configuration file, find theme section, and change its value to next

  2. Choosing between Schemes

    The NexT theme contains four different styles that you can pick: Muse, Mist, Pisces and Gemini

    You can open the _config.yml file in the NexT theme folder, locate scheme key and comment/uncomment them.

  3. Check the Theme and Scheme

    After choosing a specific scheme, let us check it out. You may need to clean the Hexo cache to avoid any potential conflicts.

    Your site is available at http://localhost:4000.
